Distinctive Use of Color:

  • Glaser's work is characterized by a bold and vibrant use of color. He often employed unexpected color combinations that created visual impact and conveyed a sense of energy. For instance, his psychedelic posters, such as the Bob Dylan poster, featured a riot of vivid hues, capturing the essence of the 1960s counterculture.

Playful and Whimsical Illustrations:

  • Glaser's illustrations often had a playful and whimsical quality. He created memorable characters and images that resonated with viewers. One of his notable works is the "Dylan" poster, where the portrait of Bob Dylan featured colorful, swirling hair, showcasing his ability to infuse a sense of fun and imagination into his designs.

Collage and Mixed Media:

  • Glaser frequently incorporated collage and mixed media techniques into his work. He combined photography, illustration, and various textures to create visually rich compositions. This eclectic approach is evident in his album covers, such as the one he designed for "Sounds of Silence" by Simon & Garfunkel, where he integrated illustration with photography to create a layered and textured artwork.
